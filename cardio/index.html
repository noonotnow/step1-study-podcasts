🎯 High-Yield Concepts

  • CO = SV × HR. Stroke volume is affected by preload (EDV), afterload (SVR), and contractility.
  • Systolic HF (HFrEF): EF <40%, dilated heart. Systolic dysfunction — can't squeeze. Treat with ACEi, β-blocker, diuretic, spironolactone.
  • Diastolic HF (HFpEF): EF preserved (>50%), stiff/hypertrophied heart. Can't relax/fill. Common in hypertension.
  • Starling's Law: ↑ preload → ↑ SV (up to a point). In HF, the curve is depressed and shifted right.
  • VSD murmur: Holosystolic, harsh, left lower sternal border. MVP: mid-systolic click → late systolic murmur.
  • Aortic stenosis: Crescendo-decrescendo systolic murmur at RUSB, radiates to carotids — syncope, angina, HF triad.
