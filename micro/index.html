🎯 High-Yield Concepts

  • Gram Positive: Thick peptidoglycan wall → stains purple. Gram Negative: thin PG + outer membrane → stains pink/red.
  • S. aureus: Catalase +, coagulase +. MRSA resistant to all β-lactams via altered PBP2a (mecA gene).
  • S. pneumoniae: Most common cause of meningitis, pneumonia, otitis media, sinusitis ("SHiNE MY SHOES" organisms = encapsulated).
  • Listeria: Gram-positive rod, tumbling motility, transmitted via unpasteurized dairy — treat with ampicillin.
  • Neisseria: Both N. meningitidis and N. gonorrhoeae are Gram-negative diplococci (oxidase +, ferment glucose).
  • Atypicals (Mycoplasma, Chlamydia, Rickettsia): No cell wall → β-lactams ineffective; treat with macrolides or doxycycline.
